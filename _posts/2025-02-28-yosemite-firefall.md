## Catching the Yosemite "Firefall"
### 28th February, 2025.

We were visiting Yosemite for the weekend, and I'd heard about this "firefall" thing - but to be honest I had relatively low expectations. We'd decided to focus on doing some big hikes, and agreed that if we were down in the valley in time we'd go and take a look!

When we drove in early in the morning there were already people setting up with cameras and we thought "that's a bit extreme, why are they here so early?". Once we got back down onto the valley floor around 4pm we realised why - it was mayhem. Stationary traffic and bus stops crowded with huge queues of people.

We decided to get on a bus, but figured we'd maybe not reach the falls in time. As the bus inched along, the clock ticked onward and everyone on the crowded bus got restless. People were checking Google Maps for how long the walk would be, and if they could make it. It was a very amicable atmosphere, with people laughing about their situation and how fast they could run 5km.

At 5pm we decided to get out of the bus in stationary traffic and make a run for it. We ran 2 miles next to the line of traffic at full speed as the light faded. I'd read that it could be spectacular - but that you needed so many things to line up: the right time of year (for the light), clear skies, low wind (so that the waterfall isn't too dispersed), and enough water flow from melting snow above etc. etc.

We arrived at a big swarm of people looking up and got our first glimpse. It seemed like we had just missed it, people were buzzing but the view was faint. It was really exciting to be there, and feel the energy - but it felt like maybe we'd missed it, or maybe this was just a strong dose of Instagram versus reality.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/yosemite-firefall-first-view.jpg" alt="First view of the firefall with faint orange glow">
  <figcaption>Our first view - the faint glow of the firefall beginning to show.</figcaption>
</figure>

I heard a park ranger say that the view was way better just down the road, so we threw the dice and kept running until we reached an even bigger swarm of people. And then it started: the crowd hushed and then started whooping and cheering as the waterfall literally came alive in bright orange. I've never seen anything like it, and it was genuinely incredible.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/yosemite-firefall.jpg" alt="Yosemite firefall glowing bright orange">
  <figcaption>The firefall in full glory. This is not photo editing magic - it really looked like this!</figcaption>
</figure>

The atmosphere was amazing, people were cheering and shouting, it was like we were all taking part in some mad ancient ritual. The photos in this post were taken on my iPhone, it really looked like this - this is not photo editing magic!

Yes there were millions of people, and yes it felt very touristy - but it was totally worth it!
