Given the three-day weekend May bank holiday, we decided to head up to Glenshiel to target the ridge and surrounding Munros. It was a long drive up on the Friday night after work, and a pretty cold night for May. This was the first camping trip of the year, so I hadn't quite calibrated layers/gear yet.

## Day 1: Saturday

We were up early on Saturday and drove a little South to do a round of three: Carn Ghluasaid, Sgurr nan Conbhairean, Sail Chaorainn. It was a slightly overcast day, but warm. When we were up on the ridge there were pockets when the sun came out and that gave us a tantalising glimpse of early Summer.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/glenshiel-DSC_7335.JPG" alt="View from the ridge on day one">

This route is relatively straightforward, but gives you some really dramatic viewpoints. There's a big drop-off after the first summit, and a really photogenic ridge that you do twice during the out-and-back.

## Day 2: The Glenshiel 7

But the main event of this trip came on Sunday: the Glenshiel 7 (Creag a' Mhaim, Druim Shionnach, Aonach air Chrith, Maol Chinn-dearg, Sgurr an Doire Leathain, Sgurr an Lochain, Creag nan Damh)! We parked near the Cluanie Inn and set off sharpish: leaving our method of return transport as a future problem (this is point to point route). It was a busy hill, we passed several groups on the way up - including a man with a very young daughter who was doing the whole ridge, incredibly impressive. Every summit brought another brilliant perspective on an epic ridgeline.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/glenshiel-DSC_7417.JPG" alt="The Glenshiel ridge">
  <figcaption>The epic Glenshiel ridgeline stretching ahead.</figcaption>
</figure>

We brewed some herbal tea at the top of the second summit which was a great first use of the new stove! After yoyoing a bit with a pair of ladies who had driven up from Glasgow that morning, they pointed out that you could see Ben Nevis in the distance. I clumsily said "oh...yeah you know what I thought that looked like Ben Nevis!", and she said "yeah that's cus it is". Said with a smile on her face, but we were laughing about that interaction for quite a while afterwards!

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/glenshiel-DSC_7526.JPG" alt="Distant mountain views">
  <figcaption>Ben Nevis visible in the distance.</figcaption>
</figure>

It's mostly a pretty broad ridge, giving you a real feeling of size and scale. But there are some narrower sections, and a few mini scrambles to keep you honest. It's nice that the scenery and summits really change as you go, it doesn't feel like one long continuous drag.

But it is a very long day out, and my knee was not doing great on the downhills, limiting our speed a bit. The views, and subconscious pressure of not wanting to be overtaken by the very young girl, kept us going! We had a quick dip in the river on the final descent and at the bottom we met Mum and child #2 of the Dad and daughter combo. They very kindly agreed to give us a lift back to Cluanie which was hugely appreciated!

## Day 3: A' Chralaig and Mullach Fraoch-choire

On our final day before driving back we did A' Chralaig and Mullach Fraoch-choire. This was an epic day! It was bright blue skies, incredibly warm, and really felt like Summer. I had no idea how cool Mullach Fraoch-choire is. The ridge on its approach, and scrambling sections up to its summit are so much fun: it's hard to believe it's in Scotland. It was an incredible way to end the trip, and easily became one of my favourite Munros.
