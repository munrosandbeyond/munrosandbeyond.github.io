## Rim to River to Rim
### 28th February, 2026.

This has been a bucket list trail run for years. I remember watching the Jim Walmsley FKT video and dreaming about running this route myself.

  <iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/T_rDA-7E_ys" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>

We planned to descend via Bright Angel Trail and climb out South Kaibab. A trail closure meant we couldn't go all the way down Bright Angel to the river, so instead we traversed along the Tonto Trail, dropped to the river via South Kaibab, and came back out the same way.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-rim.jpg" alt="View from the South Rim">
  <figcaption>Arriving at the rim. It looked like a very long way down.</figcaption>
</figure>

Arriving at the rim was incredible - a beautifully clear day with views stretching forever. It also looked like a very long way down.

We'd planned to hike, but the trails were too enticing. Before long we were running - crazy switchbacks with more and more views opening up as we descended. We passed a few hikers and a group of mules, but soon we were basically alone.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-tonto.jpg" alt="Running the Tonto Trail">
  <figcaption>The Tonto Trail - perfect floaty singletrack.</figcaption>
</figure>

The stretch along Tonto was probably my favourite. Perfect floaty singletrack, incredible views in every direction, and the feeling that there wasn't another human for miles.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-red-rock.jpg" alt="Inner canyon rock formations">
  <figcaption>The ever-changing colours of the inner canyon.</figcaption>
</figure>

Once we linked up with South Kaibab there were more people, mostly coming up. After 14+ miles of downhill running, we finally reached the river.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-bridge.jpg" alt="Crossing the bridge over the Colorado River">
  <figcaption>Crossing the Colorado River - the lowest point of the day.</figcaption>
</figure>

We made our way to Phantom Ranch. I'd read they sell lemonade but wasn't sure if they'd be open. As we approached, we spotted a lady walking with a cup in hand. We got extremely hyped.

Lemonade. With ice. In that moment, it was truly impeccable. It was roasting at the bottom - high 20s, maybe 30°C. We were both pretty dead and dehydrated, so it was great to fill up and stop for a lunch pause. The scale of the challenge ahead wasn't lost on us.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-inner-gorge.jpg" alt="View up through the inner gorge">
  <figcaption>Looking back up. A long way to go.</figcaption>
</figure>

The climb back up was long and hot. But it was stunning. South Kaibab might actually be the more beautiful trail - amazing rock formations, and the views kept changing every twenty minutes. We had one brief stop in a shade shelter, but otherwise just powered up and up.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-south-kaibab.jpg" alt="South Kaibab Trail">
  <figcaption>The South Kaibab ridge - brutal but beautiful.</figcaption>
</figure>

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/grand-canyon-climb.jpg" alt="Climbing back up to the rim">
  <figcaption>The final push back to the rim.</figcaption>
</figure>

Towards the top we hit crowds again, and enjoyed the satisfaction of zooming past them as the end came into sight.

This was a truly epic day. Descending into the unknown, with that daunting climb waiting at the end - there's something special about that kind of challenge. Bucket list: ticked.
